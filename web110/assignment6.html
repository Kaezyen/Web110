Project Management Summary

During this unit I learned how to control a project from the beggining to the end of a project. To start with figuring out the details that the stakeholders want, the costs that we need to stick to, and the timing of the project, as long as the people I would need to complete it.
The big thing that we learned during this unit was the project development cycle, which is like many other cycles when it comes to creating something but is still extremely imporant to know these are the following steps.


  • Initiating
  • Planning
  • Production/Execution
  • Monitoring and Controlling
  • Closing

Using this knowledge we have a baseline of what each project needs to go through before we can come out with something successful. Throughout my time of browing random IT related forums I found myself always finding people saying it may not be a good idea to start working with a start up company that wants to pay with stock and salary since most of them end up failing, after reviewing the videos they said only about 1/3 of projects in tech end up being completed and alot of it falls down to the project management, how the funds were allocated, how much time was wasted, etc. A good example of this is with the creators of DOOM and the arist who wrote the music for DOOM Eternal, alot of the sound tracks were messed up on the OST for the game and people were wondering what was going on, originally the company behind DOOM just blamed the artist but recently emails and screenshots came out that shows the poor planning on the part of the company. The arist said that he was not let in that they were making a soundtrack, and once he knew they were making a soundtrack he only head 2 months time remaining until the game was suppose to launch, even after that the company behind DOOM failed to give him a contract in time, as well as not let him know what songs he needs to master for the soundtrack and was simply told to master 12 songs for it, turns out the company didn't want the 12 songs he picked and asked him to restart with new songs. Without going more on this the original launch for the DOOM OST wasn't very good due to the poor communication of the project manager and this music artist, as well as the timing the company used to release the game and simply did not give the artist enough time to do the soundtrack, and there was also issues with the artist getting paid in the end after all his work. Stepping back here we see that the project manager failed in a few areas

  • Initiaing - Failed to find the scope of the project and did not know what was wanted
  • Planning - Failed to let the proper people know what they need to work on - as well as having enough time to do so
  • Execution - Follows with time restraint, still failed to produce a contract for the artist even with 2 months remaining for the project
  • Controlling - Failed to ensure the project was moving along at a timely pace and was being done correctly and up to standards
  • Closing - After it was all said and done, the company offered the artist $100,000 to keep quiet about the situation and released a soundtrack that was NOT up to standards and many customers realized it when running it through audio software to see the wave forms.

I would like to use this knowledge in the future when I am working on projects myself or if I ever find myself in a lead developer position.
